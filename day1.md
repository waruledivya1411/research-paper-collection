# Day 1 — literature set (30 papers)

Your pasted list repeated several items (same DOI more than once). This file contains **30 unique entries**: **23** from your paste, plus **7** widely cited or complementary works on in vivo electrochemistry, fast-scan cyclic voltammetry, and method comparison, so the count reaches 30.

---

### 1. Electrochemical detection of nanomolar dopamine in the presence of neurophysiological concentration of ascorbic acid and uric acid using charge-coated carbon nanotubes via facile and green preparation

- **Authors:** Oh, Jeong-Wook, et al.
- **Year:** 2016
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.talanta.2015.10.020
- **Summary:** Presents a green route to **charge-modified carbon nanotube electrodes** aimed at **dopamine (DA) detection at low nanomolar levels** when strong interferents (ascorbic acid, uric acid) are present at brain-like abundances. The emphasis is on **practical, eco-friendly surface preparation** to improve the DA signal relative to the background and competing redox events.

### 2. Electrochemical and biosensor techniques to monitor neurotransmitter changes with depression

- **Authors:** Dunham, Kelly E.; Venton, B. Jill
- **Year:** 2024
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s00216-024-05136-9
- **Summary:** Synthesizes how **electrochemistry- and enzyme-based** approaches are used to track **monoamine- and related neurochemistry** in models relevant to **depression**. The review connects method choices to what can and cannot be inferred about state-dependent transmitter dynamics.

### 3. Enzymatic electrochemical biosensors for neurotransmitters detection: recent achievements and trends

- **Authors:** Fredj, Zina, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/chemosensors11070388
- **Summary:** Surveys **enzyme-coupled electrochemical schemes** (substrate specificity plus electrode transduction) for neurotransmitters and related messengers. Highlights recent **material/immobilization** trends and the trade space between **selectivity, stability, and speed**.

### 4. Real-time in vivo detection techniques for neurotransmitters: a review

- **Authors:** Su, Yi, et al.
- **Year:** 2020
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1039/D0AN01175D
- **Summary:** Overviews **real-time, in-animal** measurement paradigms spanning multiple physical principles (e.g., electrochemistry, optical, sampling-based pipelines). Serves as a high-level map of what “real time” can mean in **temporal resolution, invasiveness, and molecular scope**.

### 5. Recent advances in the development and characterization of electrochemical and electrical biosensors for small-molecule neurotransmitters

- **Authors:** He, Jiayi, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acssensors.3c00082
- **Summary:** Focuses on **device-level advances** in **electrochemical and field-effect / impedance-style** transducers for small-molecule messengers. Emphasizes **validation practices** and how interface engineering (coatings, nanomaterials) alters **limit of detection and drift**.

### 6. An updated review on electrochemical nanobiosensors for neurotransmitter detection

- **Authors:** Choi, Hye Kyu, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/bios13090892
- **Summary:** Reviews how **nanostructured interfaces** (high surface area, tunable kinetics) are used in **neurotransmitter electrochemical sensors**. The narrative typically stresses improved sensitivity while noting challenges in **batch reproducibility and complex-matrix behavior**.

### 7. Recent developments of bioanalytical methods in determination of neurotransmitters in vivo

- **Authors:** Matys, Joanna, et al.
- **Year:** 2020
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpba.2019.113079
- **Summary:** Compiles **bioanalytical** routes (often chromatography- or MS-coupled) for **in vivo or near–in vivo** neurotransmitter quantification, alongside electrochemical options. Useful for understanding **sampling and separation bottlenecks** that define measurable panels.

### 8. A comprehensive review on neurotransmitters, its biological importance and methods of analysis

- **Authors:** Hussain, Saddam, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.52700/jmmg.v4i3.132
- **Summary:** Provides a broad, textbook-style description of **major transmitter classes, metabolism, and physiological roles** alongside common **analytical families**. Serves as a **cross-check point** for terminology and typical concentration scales before diving into sensor papers.

### 9. Micro- and nano-electrodes for neurotransmitter monitoring

- **Authors:** Marinesco, Stéphane
- **Year:** 2021
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.coelec.2021.100746
- **Summary:** Argues for **size-dependent advantages** of micro- and nanoscale electrodes in **localized chemical measurements** in tissue. The piece connects electrode geometry to **mass transport, spatial averaging, and chronic implant considerations**.

### 10. Frontiers in electrochemical sensors for neurotransmitter detection: towards measuring neurotransmitters as chemical diagnostics for brain disorders

- **Authors:** Ou, Yangguang, et al.
- **Year:** 2019
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1039/C9AY00055K
- **Summary:** Surveys how electrochemical **sensor motifs** (modified electrodes, array concepts) are being positioned toward **diagnostic-leaning** uses in **brain disorders**—framing *what* must be true for a lab measurement to support clinical questions.

### 11. Recent advances in the detection of neurotransmitters

- **Authors:** Si, Bo; Song, Edward
- **Year:** 2018
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/chemosensors6010001
- **Summary:** A compact survey of **detection families** and representative developments through the late 2010s. It is a useful entry to compare **classical electrochemistry** with emerging **optical/photonic and hybrid** approaches in one narrative.

### 12. Electrochemical biosensing of dopamine neurotransmitter: a review

- **Authors:** Lakard, Sophie, et al.
- **Year:** 2021
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/bios11060179
- **Summary:** Summarizes **dopamine-specific** electrochemical biosensor strategies, including **molecular recognition layers** and interference mitigation. The review foregrounds how **in vitro calibration success** can diverge from **biological-fluid or tissue** performance.

### 13. Recent advances in the biosensing of neurotransmitters: material and method overviews towards the biomedical analysis of psychiatric disorders

- **Authors:** Mobed, Ahmad, et al.
- **Year:** 2020
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1039/C9AY02390A
- **Summary:** Connects **materials innovation** in biosensors to **psychiatric-relevant** measurement goals (e.g., neurotransmitter panels and dynamics). The framing highlights constraints around **peripheral vs central** readouts in biomedical translation.

### 14. Latest trends in electrochemical sensors for neurotransmitters: a review

- **Authors:** Tavakolian-Ardakani, Zahra, et al.
- **Year:** 2019
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/s19092037
- **Summary:** Surveys the **trendline** in electrochemical neurotransmitter sensing: electrode chemistry, **miniaturization**, and integration concepts current to 2019. A practical companion for noticing what became “standard practice” in reporting figures-of-merit.

### 15. “Nano”: an emerging avenue in electrochemical detection of neurotransmitters

- **Authors:** Madhurantakam, Sasya, et al.
- **Year:** 2020
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acschemneuro.0c00355
- **Summary:** Argues for **nanomaterials** as levers to tune **electron-transfer kinetics and loading** in neurochemical electrochemistry. The discussion is typically anchored in the tension between **ultrasensitive bench demos** and **in vivo stability**.

### 16. Analytical strategies in neurotransmitter measurements: a mini literature review

- **Authors:** Zhu, Mingyu, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1002/bmc.5521
- **Summary:** Short tour of **separation-based and hybrid analytical strategies** for neurotransmitter quantification, often in complex matrices. Helpful for reconciling what **sensors** claim versus what **gold-standard chromatography/MS** can validate.

### 17. Carbon-based electrochemical sensors for in vivo and in vitro neurotransmitter detection

- **Authors:** Song, Huijun, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1080/10408347.2021.1997571
- **Summary:** Reviews how **carbon families** (fibers, nanotubes, doped structures) are engineered for **in vitro and in vivo** neurotransmitter electrochemistry. A useful bridge between “classic” carbon microelectrodes and more engineered carbon interfaces.

### 18. Enzymatic platforms for sensitive neurotransmitter detection

- **Authors:** Baluta, Sylwia, et al.
- **Year:** 2020
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/s20020423
- **Summary:** Focuses on **enzymatic transduction** mechanisms that translate low-abundance analytes to measurable currents or optical signals. Emphasizes **sensitivity** benefits while underlining the **enzyme-lifetime and calibration** problem class.

### 19. From enzymatic dopamine biosensors to OECT biosensors of dopamine

- **Authors:** Ravariu, Cristian
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/bios13080806
- **Summary:** Traces a technology arc from **classical enzymatic dopamine transduction** to **organic electrochemical transistor (OECT)** readouts. The comparison highlights how **ion coupled dynamics** in OECTs create different noise and bandwidth tradeoffs than amperometry.

### 20. A selected review of recent advances in the study of neuronal circuits using fiber photometry

- **Authors:** Wang, Yuanmo, et al.
- **Year:** 2021
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.pbb.2021.173113
- **Summary:** Summarizes how **fiber photometry** enables population-scale **fluorescence** readouts of circuit activity, often in tandem with **genetic sensors**. It complements electrochemical work by making explicit what optical integration buys (cell-type specificity) at the cost of different artifacts.

### 21. Nanomaterial-integrated fiber neural probes for deep brain monitoring and modulation: challenges and opportunities

- **Authors:** Zheng, Di, et al.
- **Year:** 2026
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1002/adfm.202517054
- **Summary:** Surveys next-generation **fiber neural probes** that embed **nanomaterials** to enable monitoring (and often stimulation) in deep brain targets. The emphasis is on **chronic reliability, light delivery, and interface mechanics** in heterogeneous tissue.

### 22. Significance of an electrochemical sensor and nanocomposites: toward the electrocatalytic detection of neurotransmitters and their importance within the physiological system

- **Authors:** Kaur, Harjot, et al.
- **Year:** 2023
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/acsnanoscienceau.2c00039
- **Summary:** Discusses **nanocomposite electrodes** in terms of **electrocatalysis** and sensitivity for neurotransmitter redox chemistries, tied back to **physiological concentration scales**. A useful read for connecting **materials chemistry claims** to **biofluid complexity**.

### 23. Emerging technologies for fiber-optic-based sensors in biomedical domain: a review and recent developments

- **Authors:** Sahana Bujugundla, Ravinder; Pradhan, Himansu Shekhar
- **Year:** 2024
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1109/TIM.2024.3481569
- **Summary:** Broad review of **fiber-optic** sensing paradigms in biomedicine, including transduction and interrogation methods relevant to **continuous monitoring** contexts. Serves as a photonics counterpoint to **wire-based** electrochemistry in implantable device thinking.

### 24. Detecting subsecond dopamine release with fast-scan cyclic voltammetry in vivo (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Robinson, D. L.; Venton, B. J.; Heien, M. L. A. V.; Wightman, R. M.
- **Year:** 2003
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1373/49.10.1763
- **Summary:** Foundational in vivo **fast-scan cyclic voltammetry (FSCV)** reference for **subsecond dopamine** dynamics, demonstrating how **voltammetric identity** and sampling rate enable transient tracking in behaving contexts. A baseline citation when judging newer sensors’ time resolution claims.

### 25. Higher sensitivity dopamine measurements with faster-scan cyclic voltammetry (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Keithley, R. B.; Takmakov, P.; Bucher, E. S.; Belle, A. M.; Owesson-White, C. A.; Park, J.; Wightman, R. M.
- **Year:** 2011
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/ac200143v
- **Summary:** Shows how **faster FSCV waveforms** can improve **dopamine sensitivity** in practice, with implications for detection limits in noisy tissue environments. The paper is also a good pointer to the importance of **waveform and electrode pre-treatment** as controlled variables.

### 26. Carbon-fiber microelectrodes for in vivo applications (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Huffman, M. L.; Venton, B. J.
- **Year:** 2009
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1039/b807563h
- **Summary:** Reviews how **carbon-fiber microelectrodes (CFMEs)** are prepared and used for **in vivo neurochemical** measurements, including common modification routes. A canonical bridge between “materials for electrodes” and “measurements in tissue.”

### 27. In-vivo electrochemistry: what can we learn about living systems? (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Wilson, G. S.; Johnson, M. A.
- **Year:** 2008
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/cr068082i
- **Summary:** A **Chemical Reviews**-level treatment of in vivo and implantable electrochemistry, foregrounding what electrochemical readouts can reveal about **physiology, biocompatibility, and drift**. It helps contextualize modern nanosensor papers against long-standing in vivo measurement constraints.

### 28. A review of neurotransmitters sensing methods for neuro-engineering research (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Niyonambaza, S. D.; Kumar, P.; Xing, P.; Mathault, J.; De Koninck, P.; Boisselier, E.; Boukadoum, M.; Miled, A.
- **Year:** 2019
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.3390/app9214719
- **Summary:** Positions neurotransmitter **sensing** within **neuro-engineering** decision-making, comparing **electrochemistry, sampling/imaging, and nano-object** approaches. Useful as a “choose your modality” map when a research question spans signal sources and time scales.

### 29. Dopamine detection with fast-scan cyclic voltammetry used with analog background subtraction (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Hermans, A.; Keithley, R. B.; Kita, J. M.; Sombers, L. A.; Wightman, R. M.
- **Year:** 2008
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/ac800108j
- **Summary:** Presents a hardware/data pathway (**analog background subtraction**) to improve FSCV dopamine signals by reducing **large background components** in living tissue. A concrete methods paper for students implementing FSCV acquisition stacks.

### 30. Resolving neurotransmitters detected by fast-scan cyclic voltammetry (added to complete a set of 30 unique papers)

- **Authors:** Heien, M. L. A. V.; Johnson, M. A.; Wightman, R. M.
- **Year:** 2004
- **Link:** https://doi.org/10.1021/ac0491509
- **Summary:** Develops a **multivariate / chemometric** approach to **separate overlapping** contributions in FSCV data, enabling more confident assignment when multiple redox-active species co-vary. This is the “analysis stack” complement to the physics of electrode design.
